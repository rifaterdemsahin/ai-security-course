Core Defense Strategies

üõ°Ô∏è Adversarial Training - Turning Attacks into Model Strength

The Immunity Principle: Turn an AI's greatest weakness into its greatest strength.
  • "Vaccinating" your model against attacks
  • Generating adversarial examples for training
  • Augmenting training data with adversarial samples
  • Trade-offs between robustness and accuracy
  • Best practices for implementation
Core Principle: By exposing the model to attacks during training, you build inherent resilience that persists through deployment. This is proactive security, not reactive patching.

üö™ Input Sanitization - Your First Line of Defense

The Gatekeeper Strategy: Boost security with surprisingly simple methods.
  • Input validation techniques
  • Preprocessing methods to detect anomalies
  • Statistical outlier detection
  • Image compression and denoising as defenses
  • Implementing sanitization pipelines
Practical Advantage: Provides immediate and powerful protection without requiring complex changes to your model's architecture. It's a lightweight defense that can be deployed quickly.

üîí Differential Privacy - Protecting Data, Preserving Insight

Privacy by Design: Achieve powerful insights and strong privacy simultaneously.
  • Mathematical foundation of differential privacy
  • How noise injection protects individual data points
  • The privacy-utility trade-off
  • Practical implementation in training pipelines
  • Real-world examples (Apple's iOS keyboard)
Critical Understanding: Differential privacy allows you to confidently use datasets while guaranteeing the anonymity of the people within them. This isn't just about compliance‚Äîit's about building trustworthy AI.
